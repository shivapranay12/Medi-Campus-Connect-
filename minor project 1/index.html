"Centennial Health Centre" is a 20-bedded Hospital on campus. It caters to the health requirements of students, faculty, and residents of the campus. It also consists of a fully equipped dental facility and a pharmacy. The health centre provides its own ambulance service and offers round-the-clock healthcare facilities on campus.

Vision

"To be the primary choice of Healthcare for our community, setting the standard for excellence in student-centered care, innovation, and community engagement. We aspire to create a dynamic healthcare environment that not only addresses the immediate health needs of our students but also empowers them to lead healthy, fulfilling lives through education, prevention, and compassionate support."

Mission

"To be a provider of high-quality, patient-focused healthcare that is readily accessible, cost-effective, and meets the diverse needs of our community. We are committed to delivering compassionate care that not only treats illness but also promotes wellness and empowers individuals to actively participate in their health journey. Through continuous innovation, collaboration, and a dedication to excellence, we strive to exceed expectations and improve the overall well-being of our community."

Sevier Symptoms

Emergency symptoms that warrant contacting a doctor include:

  • Fever exceeding 103°F (39.4°C)
  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Chest pain
  • Persistent vomiting
  • Severe headache or stiff neck

If any of these symptoms occur,contact doctor immediately

Diet
  • Eat light, nutritious foods that are easy to digest, such as fruits, vegetables, soups, and broths. Avoid heavy, greasy foods that can be hard to digest.
Precautions
  • Elevated body temperature : Stay hydrated, dress lightly, and use fans or AC.
  • Sweating : Wear loose clothing, avoid hot environments, and use antiperspirants.
  • Chills and shivering : Keep warm with blankets, drink warm beverages, and use heating pads
  • Headache : Rest in a quiet place, apply cold compresses, and practice relaxation techniques.
  • Muscle aches : Avoid strenuous activity, use heat packs, and try gentle stretching.

Common Symptoms
  • Dry cough
  • Wet cough with mucus
  • Sore throat
  • Hoarseness
  • Chest discomfort
  • Fatigue

Severe Symptoms

Apologies for the oversight. Here are five emergency symptoms of a cough that warrant contacting a doctor:

  • High fever (above 103°F or 39.4°C)
  • Severe dehydration
  • Blood in mucus or sputum
  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Persistent or worsening cough lasting more than three weeks
Diet
  • Drink plenty of fluids such as water, herbal tea, and broths to stay hydrated and soothe the throat. Eat light, nutritious foods like fruits, vegetables, and soups. Honey and warm beverages can help soothe a sore throat and reduce coughing, and stay away from caffeine and alcohol.
Precautions
  • Elevated body temperature: Stay hydrated, dress lightly, and use fans or AC.
  • Sweating: Wear loose clothing, avoid hot environments, and use antiperspirants.
  • Chills and shivering: Keep warm with blankets, drink warm beverages, and use heating pads
  • Headache: Rest in a quiet place, apply cold compresses, and practice relaxation techniques.
Common Symptoms
  • Dull, aching pain in the lower back
  • Sharp, shooting pain that radiates down the leg (sciatica)
  • Muscle stiffness and tightness in the lower back
  • Limited range of motion
  • Pain that worsens with movement or prolonged sitting

Severe Symptoms

Apologies for the oversight. Here are five emergency symptoms of back pain that warrant contacting a doctor:

  • Severe pain that does not improve with rest
  • Loss of bowel or bladder control
  • Weakness, numbness, or tingling in the legs
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fever accompanying back pain
Diet
  • For back pain, follow these dietary guidelines: Maintain a balanced diet rich in anti-inflammatory foods such as fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water. Avoid processed foods, sugary drinks, and excessive caffeine. Omega-3 fatty acids found in fish and flaxseed can help reduce inflammation. These tips can help manage symptoms and support recovery.
Precautions
  • Avoid heavy lifting: Use proper lifting techniques or seek assistance.
  • Maintain good posture: Sit and stand with a straight back and shoulders.
  • Regular exercise: Engage in low-impact activities like walking or swimming to strengthen back muscles.
  • Use ergonomic furniture: Ensure your workspace supports good posture and reduces strain on your back.
Common Symptoms
  • Nausea
  • Vomting
  • Diarrhea
  • Abdominal pain and or cramps
  • Fever

Sevier Symptoms

Apologies for the oversight. Here are five emergency symptoms of food poisoning that warrant contacting a doctor: Severe dehydration

  • High Fever
  • severe dehydration
  • Blood in vomit or stools
  • Persistent vomiting
  • signs of neurologica issues
Diet
  • For nausea, eat small, bland meals like crackers and sip ginger tea. With vomiting, start with clear liquids, then try bananas, rice, applesauce, and toast (BRAT diet). For diarrhea, stay hydrated and eat binding foods like bananas and rice. To ease abdominal pain, eat easily digestible foods like mashed potatoes and avoid spicy or fatty foods. For fever, drink plenty of fluids and eat light, nutritious foods like fruits and vegetables.
Precautions
  • Nausea and Vomiting: Sip clear fluids, avoid solid foods until vomiting stops, and try ginger tea or crackers.
  • Diarrhea: Stay hydrated with electrolyte solutions, avoid dairy and fatty foods, and eat bland foods like bananas, rice, applesauce, and toast.
  • Abdominal Pain: Apply a warm compress to your stomach, avoid spicy foods, and eat small, frequent meals.
Common Symptoms
  • Redness
  • Swelling
  • Warmth in the affected area
  • Pain or tenderness
  • Pus or discharge

Severe Symptoms

Apologies for the oversight. Here are five emergency symptoms of skin infections that warrant contacting a doctor: Severe spreading of redness or swelling

  • High Fever
  • Severe pain or swelling
  • Red streaks leading away from the infection
  • Persistent pus or discharge
  • Signs of systemic infection like chills or fatigue
Diet
  • For promoting healing, eat a diet rich in vitamins and minerals. Include foods high in Vitamin C (citrus fruits, berries), zinc (nuts, seeds), and protein (lean meats, legumes) to support tissue repair. Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water and avoid excessive sugar or processed foods.
Precautions
  • Redness and Swelling: Keep the area clean and dry, apply a cool compress to reduce swelling, and elevate the affected area if possible.
  • Warmth and Pain: Use over-the-counter pain relief medications as needed, and apply an antibiotic ointment if recommended by a healthcare provider.
  • Pus or Discharge: Avoid squeezing or picking at the infected area, cover it with a clean bandage, and seek medical advice if it persists or worsens.
